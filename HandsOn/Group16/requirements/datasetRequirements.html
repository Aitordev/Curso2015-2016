We chose two datasets that are very similar to each other. These are the datasets for London Employment Jobs series = Employee Jobs + Self-Employed Jobs and Borough Employee Jobs series = Employee Jobs only.

These two datasets are both related to a great smart city, London http://data.london.gov.uk/. London city has much more linked data available online in csv format, that is why we picked these two datasets so we could enhance our project with any of the published linked data of the city.

Following the instructions provided in http://www.nationalarchives.gov.uk/doc/open-government-licence/version/2/ we are able to: copy, publish, distribute and transmit the Information; adapt the Information; exploit the Information commercially and non-commercially for example, by combining it with other Information, or by including it in our own product or application, but we must acknowledge the source of the Information by including any attribution statement specified by the Information Provider(s) and, where possible, provide a link to this licence.

The data that we are dealing with comes from two different data sets. These data are related to the amount of jobs per sector in London city. Additional information can be found in: http://www.london.gov.uk/sites/default/files/wp52.pdf

Finally, we chose these data because it can be easily linkable with other cities, jobs information over the years, business over the year, comparisons with other cities jobes and other similar topics.
